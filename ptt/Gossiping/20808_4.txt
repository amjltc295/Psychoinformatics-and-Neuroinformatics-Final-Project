Former MotoGP champion Nicky Hayden has died aged 35, five days after being
involved in a crash while cycling.

The American collided with a car on the Rimini coastline in Italy on
Wednesday, 17 May.

He suffered &#34;serious cerebral damage&#34; and had been in the intensive care unit
of Cesena&#39;s Maurizio Bufalini Hospital.

Hayden, who competed in the World Superbike Championship in Italy on 14 May,
won the MotoGP championship in 2006.

A hospital statement issued on Thursday said Hayden had suffered &#34;a serious
polytrauma&#34;, which is a medical term to describe the condition of a person
who has multiple traumatic injuries.

Hayden&#39;s title triumph

The Kentucky-born racer first competed in MotoGP in 2003 and finished third
in the standings two years later. He ended Valentino Rossi&#39;s five-year
winning streak in 2006 following a dramatic final race in Valencia.

Hayden had been eight points adrift of Rossi heading into the decider, but
saw the Italian slide out on lap five and eventually finish in 13th place.
Hayden&#39;s third-place finish allowed him to take the title by five points.

He remains the last American to win the premier class of motorcycle road
racing.

At the time, BBC commentator Steve Parrish described the season as &#34;the most
entertaining I have ever seen&#34;.
